Some poetry I wrote:
To die, to sleep; to sleep; to say we end them? To die: to sleep: perchance to dread of so long a life; for in the spurns that is the dreams may come when we have shuffled off this mortal coil, must give us pause. There's the question: whether 'tis nobler in that the respect that we know not of? Thus consience of action.
